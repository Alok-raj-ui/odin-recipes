Recipe of samosa

HERE'S THE PROCESS

Ingredients:

For the filling:

  • 2 cups boiled potatoes (mashed)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, beans), boiled
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (adjust to taste) Salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro (coriander leaves), chopped
  • 1-2 tbsp lemon juice (optional) 2 tbsp oil for cooking

    • For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup oil or melted ghee (clarified butter) 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water, as needed

For frying:

Instructions:
  1. Prepare the filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies, sauté for another minute.
    • Add turmeric powder, ground coriander, chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
    • Add the boiled mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, beans) and mash the mixture gently. Stir everything together.
    • Add the mashed potatoes and salt. Mix thoroughly, ensuring the spices coat the filling well.
    • Stir in the chopped cilantro and lemon juice. Remove from heat and let it cool down.
  2. Make the dough:
    • In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and oil (or ghee).
    • Gradually add water to make a firm and smooth dough. Knead for about 5-7 minutes.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Shape the samosas:
    • Divide the dough into small balls (about 10-12).
    • Roll each ball into a small oval or round shape, about 6-7 inches in diameter.
    • Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.
    • Shape each semi-circle into a cone by folding it in half, sealing the edge with a little water.
    • Fill the cone with the cooled filling and pinch the open edge to seal the samosa into a triangular shape. Make sure the filling is secured tightly.
  4. Fry the samosas:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
    • Gently drop the samosas into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding the pan.
    • Fry the samosas, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove from oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve hot with tamarind chutney or mint chutney.

Enjoy your crispy, flavorful samosas!
